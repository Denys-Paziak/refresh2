What types of fabrics and materials do you accept for dry cleaning?

Regular Cleaning: Always clean off dirt and dust from your shoes using a soft brush or cloth. This helps in preserving the material, whether it's leather, suede, or fabric.

Weatherproofing: For leather and suede shoes, consider using a weatherproof spray to protect them from rain, snow, and other elements.

Conditioning: If you have leather shoes, regularly apply a leather conditioner to keep them moisturized, preventing cracks and dryness.

Rotate Your Shoes: Avoid wearing the same pair every day. Giving shoes time to air out can help prevent odors and reduce wear.

Shoe Trees: Use cedar shoe trees when you're not wearing your shoes. They help in retaining the shape of the shoe and absorb any moisture.

Avoid Wet Environments: If your shoes do get wet, let them dry naturally and never put them near direct heat, as this can damage the material.

Storage: Store shoes in a cool, dry place and avoid stacking them on top of one another, which can cause them to lose their shape.

Heel and Sole: Check the heels and soles regularly for wear. It's easier to replace a worn sole than to buy new shoes.

Mind Your Walking Habits: How you walk can affect the wear of your shoes, especially the heel area. Being mindful of your walking habits can prevent uneven wear.

Visit a Professional: If you notice any damage, take your shoes to a professional for repairs before the issue gets worse.
